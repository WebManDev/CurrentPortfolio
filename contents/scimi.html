Software Development Intern at Princeton Eviction Lab

During my tenure at the Princeton Eviction Lab, I undertook extensive research and data collection concerning homeless shelters. This involved not only gathering raw data but also processing and analyzing it to extract meaningful insights. I gained substantial experience in geocoding, transforming addresses into geographic coordinates to facilitate spatial analysis. Additionally, I honed my skills in data scraping, automating the extraction of relevant information from various online sources.

One of my significant achievements was the creation of a sophisticated two-layer map using R. This map visually represented the spatial distribution and characteristics of homeless shelters, integrating multiple data sources to provide a comprehensive overview. This work required a strong grasp of spatial data analysis, as well as proficiency in R programming for data manipulation and visualization. Through this project, I contributed to a deeper understanding of the geographic dimensions of homelessness and the resources available to address it.
